Christos Papachristos

Head Coach

Christos Papachristos (born February 11, 1959) is a Greek track coach and former international-class middle and long-distance runner. Born in Trikala, after finishing high school he moved in Athens to study Physical and Sport Science at University of Athens. At the same time Papachristos was a professional athlete competing in National, European and World Championships. He is best known for his performance taking 5th place in the Marathon of the 1987 World Championship in Seoul. Papachristos held Greek track records of 3:38.30 for 1500m (1980 - Athens) lasting for 17 years and 7:48.34 for 3000m (1983 â€“ Oslo). He also set the Balkan and Greek record 3:41.67 for indoor 1500m (1982 - Milan). In total Papachristos has won 17 balkan medals.

After his competition career, Papachristos moved into coaching in 1987. By now this included middle distance runner Panayotis Stroubakos, Olympian in 2000 and 2004, current National holder for 800m (1:45.00) and 1500m (3:36.74). Long distance runner Maria Protopappa, Olympian in 2004 and current National holder for 5000m (15:04.03). Elena Filandra, Olympian in 2004, finished fourth at Mediterranean Games, current National holder for indoor 800m (2:02.08) with a personal best of 2:00.44 for outdoor 800m. Filandra is also the only Greek female middle distance qualifier in semi-finals of Olympic Games. Middle distance runner Konstantinos Nakopoulos, current National holder for indoor 800m (1:48.64). George Giannelis, Olympian in 2000 with PB 8:26.67 for 3000m steeplechase. Long distance runner Antonios Papantonis, fourth at European Championships U23 (PB 29:14.43). For 20 years Papachristos was the distance running coach of Greek National Team. At present he is the head coach of the Papachristos Running Team.
